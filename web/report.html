Missed-Us Threshold Report (2026)

Analysis of how long to wait after the first cohort siren before declaring “missed us” (כנראה עבר) for each city.
Based on historical pre-alert events from the israel-alerts-data dataset.

How it works

When a PRE_ALERT arrives, other cities in the same alert (“cohort”) may receive sirens. The threshold is the number of seconds after the first cohort siren to wait before declaring the event “probably passed” for the target city.

  • miss = target city never got a siren (cohort may or may not have)
  • immediate = target city got hit on the same siren wave as cohort (no gap)
  • hit after gap = target city got hit N seconds after the first cohort siren

The threshold is the minimum wait (in 30s increments, up to 1200s) such that ≤5% of amber declarations would be false (i.e., target city actually gets a siren afterward).
